Paneer Sabzi Recipe


Description

Paneer based curries have always been a top favourite for most of the people from a vegetarian Indian background. These curries are usually fancy and may require extra spices and ingredients to make a perfect fancy paneer curry. However, you can also make a simple no fuss paneer ki sabzi with minimal ingredients available in your kitchen, which will match any restaurant style curry.

Ingredients

  • 15 cubes paneer
  • Turmeric
  • Chili powder
  • Ginger garlic
  • Salt
  • Ghee
  • Oil
  • Garam masala
  • Kasuri methi

Steps

  1. Firstly, in a large bowl take 15 cubes paneer, ¼ tsp turmeric, ½ tsp chili powder, ½ tsp ginger garlic paste and ¼ tsp salt.
  2. Mix all the spices well. Cover and rest for 30 minutes or more for marination.
  3. Now heat 3 tsp oil and fry the marinated paneer.
  4. Do not overcook paneer as it turns rubbery. Keep aside.
  5. Heat 1 tbsp ghee and 2 tbsp oil in a large pan.
  6. Add 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 bay leaf, 1 pod black cardamom, 1 inch cinnamon, 2 pods cardamom and 4 cloves.
  7. Saute on low flame till the spices become aromatic.
  8. Now add 1 onion, 1 tsp ginger garlic paste and fry the onion till it becomes golden brown.
  9. Further keeping the flame on low, add ¼ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp chili powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, ½ tsp cumin powder, ½ tsp garam masala and 1 tsp salt.
  10. Saute on low flame till the spices become aromatic.
  11. Add 2 cups tomato puree and mix well.
  12. Cover and cook for 10 minutes or till the oil separates.
  13. Keeping the flame on low, add ½ cup curd and stir continuously.
  14. Keep cooking until the curd is well mixed.
  15. Now add 1 cup water and mix well adjusting the consistency as required.
  16. Further, add the cooked paneer and mix well.
  17. Cover and simmer for 5 minutes or until the flavours are absorbed well.
  18. Add 1 tsp kasuri methi, 2 tbsp coriander and mix well.
  19. Finally, enjoy your paneer ki sabzi with roti or rice. :)
